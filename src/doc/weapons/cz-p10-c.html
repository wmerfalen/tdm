CZ P-10 C is a compact semi-automatic striker-fired pistol made by Česká zbrojovka (CZ) in the Czech Republic. It was introduced in 2017 after development that began in 2014.[1] The weapon is designed for self-defense and use in the armed forces (although there is no reference to usage by official armed forces of any country as of 2018).

CZ P-10 C has a mechanically and thermally stable polymer frame reinforced with glass fiber and three interchangeable backstraps.[2] Magazines are backward compatible with CZ P-07 but not vice versa.[3]

CZ P-10 C is a direct competitor to Glock 19.[4] The guns have very similar characteristics but Glock is about 2.4 oz lighter, is about a tenth of inch shorter and has a different grip angle.[5]

History[edit]

The P-10 C is the first striker-fired pistol manufactured by CZ.[6] The C in the name stands for compact.[7] Ergonomics of the P-10 C are similar to that of the CZ 75 with a newly engineered trigger system to make it "more efficient and easier to handle."[8]

Design details[edit]

The P-10 C is a striker-fired semi-automatic pistol. This type of trigger system prevents the firearm from discharging unless the trigger is fully depressed, even if the pistol is dropped. Other safety features include a firing pin block which mechanically obstructs the firing pin. This pistol does not feature a magazine disconnect which prevents it from being fired when the magazine is withdrawn.[9]

The frame is made out of a fiber-reinforced polymer while the slide is made of stainless steel. The slide and magazine releases are ambidextrous. The barrel is cold hammer-forged steel with a black nitride finish.[9] The pistol sights are iron with a three-dot system that become night sights after being exposed to light.[8]

The trigger guard is large to fit a variety of finger widths and to allow for shooting while wearing gloves. The trigger guard is undercut for a more ergonomic fit.[10] Additional ergonomic features include three different backstrap sizes, stippling on all four sides of the grip and near the slide release, and a "deep saddle just underneath of the slide."[8]

The P-10 C factory trigger has a short reset which is tactile and audible.[9] The trigger press is rated at 4.5 lbf (20.0 N).[10]

A new P-10 C pistol from CZ comes in a lockable plastic case with a carrying handle, three backstraps, two 15-round magazines, a cable lock, cleaning brush and rod, and the owner's manual. The grips are polymer and come in three different sizes; they slide on a rail and a pin locks the grip to the frame.[11]

Variants[edit]

In September 2018, reporters got a look at a new model of the P-10 C pistol. This new version is optics ready but has a plate available to cover the milling if no optics are added. The rear sights and viewfinder were updated for better shooting accuracy. And, the bilateral magazine release buttons have been replaced by one larger button that can be reversed.[12]

CZ introduced a full size model (P-10 F) and subcompact (P-10 S) of the P-10 in October 2018. While the P-10C has a 4.02 inch barrel, the full size model has a 4.5 inch barrel and the subcompact has a 3.5 inch barrel. The full size model holds 19+1 cartridges and the subcompact holds 12+1 cartridges.[13] Like the new P-10 C model introduced in September, they both are optics ready.[14]

Awards[edit]

The CZ P-10 C has been awarded the Pistol of the Year 2017 prize by Guns & Ammo magazine.[20]

Popular culture[edit]

The P-10C is a weapon available for use in the tactical shooter game Rainbow Six Siege.[21] This handgun was introduced in the Operation Grim Sky expansion for use by operator Clash as one of her secondary weapons.[22] Although in-game the weapon has no manufacturer, it is presumed to be a variant of the CZ P-10 C.[23]
